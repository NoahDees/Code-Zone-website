Welcome to our sources page! Obviously we don't know everything ourselves and had to start somewhere on our path to learning. The references we have below are some of the places we thought had the most information or are great places to have conversations and ask more questions. Of course, you are always welcome to ask us any questions about our site or HTML from our contact page too!

  • World Wide Web Consortium
  • The W3C is the main international standards organization for the World Wide Web. Any questions you have about rules or standards can be answered by them!

  • W3 Schools
  • This website has anything you could ever want to know about web design and the many factors that go into making an amazing site (and so much more)! They even include examples and sample code that you can run and test to see how it works and how to implement it yourself.

  • Stack Overflow
  • Stack Overflow is the place to go when you have an error you don't understand, a question about how to implement a specific thing or to just learn from others. It's a question and answer site for beginner and long-time programmers alike! Almost anything you want to know has been answered, and if it's not you can ask it yourself and wait for the masses to work together to help!

  • Terry Morris
  • If you're a reader, then Terry Morris is the person to turn to. She is the author of countless Web Development textbooks, all of which are packed with tons of knowledge. Her books are easy to read and include lots of practice problems and example code to help you learn!
